Emergency Department

Regional Anaesthesia (Nerve Block) Patient Advice

You have received a nerve block. This means we injected local anaesthetic near a nerve to stop you feeling pain. Your limb or body area will feel completely numb and heavy.

Protect the Numb Area

  • Protect from heat: You will not feel burns. Keep the numb area away from radiators, hot water bottles, and hot drinks.
  • Protect from pressure: You will not feel pressure damage. Change position regularly. Do not rest the limb on hard edges.
  • Protect from injury: Use your sling or splint if provided. Do not use the numb arm or leg to support your weight. Do not walk on a numb leg.

Pain Relief

The numbness will wear off in 6 to 18 hours. Take your prescribed pain relief tablets before the numbness wears off completely. If you wait until the pain returns, it will be much harder to control.

When to return to the Emergency Department

Return immediately if you experience:

  • Ringing in your ears, a metallic taste in your mouth, or blurred vision.
  • Muscle twitches, severe agitation, or confusion.
  • The block lasts longer than 48 hours without improvement.
  • Severe pain returning rapidly and completely uncontrolled by tablets.
